Edge Bundling - Resolve Bundling Ambiguity


     The edge bundling technique is used directly for bundling adjacency edges, which may occur the bundling ambiguity problems showed in (a). The bundle in (a) might contain each edge depicted in (b). However, the real edges are probably a subset all the edges illustrated in (b). These problems can be reduced by diminishing the bundling strength that effectively controls the amount of bundling by straightening the spline curves. As shown in (c), a Fairly high bundling strength (Î² = 0.8) can be chosen to retain visual bundles while still resolving bundling ambiguity (Actually there are only 3 edges). Note these three figures are redrawn by us from the original figures shown in Holten's works:


Danny Holten. Hierarchical Edge Bundles: Visualization of Adjacency Relations in Hierarchical Data. In IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics, VOL. 12, NO. 5, SEPTEMBER/OCTOBER 2006.
